## Story 1 — Leading 30+ electricians nationwide (COMEX SA) · PRIMARY

**Situation.** At COMEX SA (Gdańsk, 2008–2016) I ran the guaranteed-power-supply (UPS) service across Poland: about 2,000 UPS units under continuous care, serviced by 10 internal technicians and 20+ external electrician/subcontractor teams. Experience levels were mixed and the operation ran under short SLAs — 2 hours to respond at central units and 4 hours to deliver replacement equipment on failure.

**Task.** As Head of Service, ensure safe, reliable delivery against those SLAs without overloading senior electricians or losing technical standards, and keep management informed with facts.

**Action.** I set clear daily priorities; coached technicians on diagnostics; defined escalation paths; tracked KPIs/SLA per team and per vendor in our in-house CMMS and CRM; distributed workload fairly across the country; and reported to the board with facts — failures, repeat jobs, parts.

**Result.** **98% SLA met** at national scale against a 2h-response / 4h-replacement target — stable, accountable delivery without overloading the team. This is the leadership model I would bring to Electrical Utilities at Lommel, adapted to the site phase and English-led communication.

**Interview angle:** "This is how I lead electricians. I'd adapt it to Ciner Glass site rules and utilities priorities."

## Story 2 — Production-critical fault on battery systems (Northvolt) · TECHNICAL DEPTH

**Situation.** At Northvolt Poland (Gdańsk, 2020–2023) I maintained production-critical electrical/electronic systems — battery power modules, DC up to 900 V — in a high-tech plant, under pressure to restore line availability fast.

**Task.** Restore the equipment safely and stop the fault from coming back, not just patch it.

**Action.** Structured 5-Why root-cause analysis; documented the failure and the fix; added a preventive check into the maintenance routine; ran **Kaizen** continuous-improvement during the production setup; coordinated across **production, quality, after-sales and warehouse** to restore line continuity — a role that demanded daily cross-functional cooperation and leading mixed-background groups.

**Result.** **Reduced repair time (MTTR) by ~65%** on the affected production line, turning a repeat firefight into structural prevention. Demonstrates the fault-analysis the team-leader role expects, plus rarer DC experience.

## Story 6 — Lockout/Tagout discipline (Northvolt & Lommel) · SAFETY

**Situation.** Electrical work on live production lines — both daily at Northvolt and now on the Lommel site — where any change to a line carries an energy-isolation risk.

**Task.** Make improvements/repairs on the line without exposing anyone to stored or live energy.

**Action.** Standard LOTO every day: isolate the area, lock and **tag** it, verify everything is safe (de-energised and checked), do the work, then bring it back together and restore the line to service only after confirmation.

**Result.** Safe, repeatable energy control as routine — not an exception. This is the permit/LOTO discipline I'd hold the team to as team leader: "fast, but never around the procedure."
