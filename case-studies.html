Case Studies

STAR format · Lead with COMEX · Teamleader Electrical Utilities

Łukasz Klimowski | Interview preparation

Priority story: COMEX leadership (30+ electricians). Support with Ciner Glass Lommel site insight (technician role) and technical production examples.

1. Leading 30+ electricians — COMEX SA (primary)

Situation Nationwide industrial service operation; ~10 internal technicians and 20+ external electrician/subcontractor teams; mixed experience levels; pressure on response time and first-time fix quality.

Task As Head of Service, ensure safe, reliable delivery without losing technical standards or overloading senior electricians.

Action Daily priority planning; coaching on diagnostics; clear escalation paths; KPI and SLA tracking per team/vendor; fair workload distribution; management reporting with facts (failures, repeat jobs, parts).

Result Stable service performance and improved accountability — demonstrates teamleader skills with experienced electricians, directly transferable to Electrical Utilities at Lommel.

Interview angle: “This is how I lead electricians — I would adapt it to Ciner Glass Lommel site rules and utilities priorities.”

2. On-site utilities work — Ciner Glass, Lommel (current, technician)

Situation Ciner Glass greenfield site, Lommel, Belgium; Electrical Utilities department during construction phase.

Task Execute assigned on-site electrical utilities work safely and correctly — not to lead the team.

Action Follow instructions from team leads; complete diverse site tasks; respect VCA/BA5 and permit rules; learn systems and workflows from the floor.

Result Up-to-date practical knowledge of the site — honest basis for teamleader credibility (“I know what technicians face daily”).

Do not overclaim: You coordinate nothing today; you understand the floor.

3. Subcontractor repeat failures — COMEX SA

Situation External electrician team repeat failures; SLA risk.

Action Failure history; Pareto; joint corrective plan; coaching on diagnostic standards; vendor KPI follow-up.

Result Lower repeat rate — example of performance management without micromanaging skilled trades.

4. Production fault — Northvolt Poland

Situation Production-critical electrical fault; pressure for fast restart.

Action 5 Why RCA; documentation; preventive check in routine.

Result Structural prevention — supports storingsanalyse expectation in teamleader role.

5. Escalation to Quality/R&D — Prodrive Eindhoven

Recurring failures; Pareto evidence; engineering-led fix — cross-department cooperation.
