KPI & Statistical Method

How regular measurement drives better utilities performance

Łukasz Klimowski | Tools: Excel · Power BI · Python · SQL

I work in a statistical way: measure → detect pattern → act → verify. Without periodic checking, repeat faults look like “bad luck”; with KPIs, they become manageable system behaviour. Beyond standard KPIs I also use TPM and, where a parameter must stay within range, statistical process control (SPC) with upper/lower control limits. Illustrative charts below show the type of impact I aim for (based on COMEX and consulting practice — not Ciner Glass confidential data).

Data serves the team — it is not a control tool. Electricians dislike paperwork, and rightly distrust metrics used to police minutes. So I am explicit: I collect data to help the floor, not to rank people. A repeat-fault Pareto exists to prove to management that a component must be modernised in the budget — because it keeps breaking and making the technicians’ work harder. KPIs win them spare parts, prevention and quieter shifts.

“How would you get a tired night-shift technician to report data honestly?” By making reporting take seconds, by showing the team what their data changed (a fixed recurring fault, a won budget item), and by never using it against them. People report when the data visibly works for them.

3. Constant checking → system quality

Weekly KPI → Team sees trend → Focus on top 3 → Less firefighting → Higher trust

Electricians benefit because priorities are data-backed, not changed every hour. Management benefits because utilities performance is visible before production stops.

4. What I would implement at Ciner Glass Lommel

  • One-page weekly utilities dashboard (print + screen).
  • Asset/zone tagging aligned to PC-10, PC-20, Utility, Batch, warehouses.
  • Separate tracking for continuity assets: UPS, generator, LPG chain.
  • SCADA vs non-SCADA fault codes — avoid mixing control issues with power supply issues.
  • Team board in electrical workshop — electricians see their own improvement (not only blame).
