Leadership Advantages

Why my profile fits Lommel utilities — beyond the job title

Łukasz Klimowski | Target: Teamleader Electrical Utilities · Ciner Glass

Comparison is not about criticising colleagues — it is about what I would add if appointed teamleader: support for independent electricians, holistic utilities thinking, and statistical follow-up on a complex greenfield site.

1. Working with independent electricians (core strength)

At COMEX, 30+ people — mostly experienced electricians who work independently — servicing 2,000 UPS units nationwide at 98% SLA. My approach is servant leadership: I start from “How can I help?” and remove blockers rather than stand over people. In practice:

Trust craft, control system

I do not tell a senior electrician how to hold a meter — I agree on outcome, safety, and deadline.

Remove blockers

Parts, permits, priorities, and management noise filtered before they hit the floor.

Fair workload

Rotations across heavy zones (PC-10 / PC-20 / continuity systems) — no burnout pockets.

Feedback with data

Performance talks use logs and KPIs — not personal opinion.

2. What I see today — and how I would develop it

As an electrician on site I see that field staff often lack structured backing from management layers: electricians solve organisational gaps alone. I am also developing — learning when to escalate earlier and how to document for the next shift.

As teamleader I would turn that observation into process:

  • Daily visible plan and single point of contact for utilities electricians.
  • Weekly KPI review with the team present — transparency builds trust.
  • Escalation paths to specialists (UPS, generator, LPG, SCADA) — Utilities leads power, not every control detail.
  • Recognition when repeat faults drop — morale matters on long site phases.

3. People development — scarce market, grow internally

Ultra-modern Lommel utilities: few external candidates have done all systems. My COMEX advantage + proposed competence matrix & mentoring model builds specialists inside the team instead of waiting for impossible hires.
