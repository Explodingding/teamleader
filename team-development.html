Team Development Model

Cooperative supervision · competence mapping · mentoring · multicultural Lommel team

Łukasz Klimowski | Proposal — Teamleader Electrical Utilities · Ciner Glass

How I ran COMEX (30+ electricians) and how I would develop the Utilities team at Lommel: cooperation between people, not top-down control alone; early competence mapping; knowledge retention through mentoring; and inclusive leadership in a multicultural team while Dutch matures on site.

2. Multicultural team — risk and my approach

Utilities today is international: Dutch colleagues, Turkish background, Europeans on English while learning Dutch, colleagues from Brazil, and more. Informal grouping by cultural circle happens — it is natural but can limit knowledge flow.

  • Do not fight cultural circles — redirect them through mixed pairs, shared KPI board, and zone rotation.
  • Official communication — Dutch where required; technical clarity in English when needed; always documented.
  • Honest with management: I will not pretend fluent Dutch tomorrow — I offer English leadership experience + fast NL progress + everything else (COMEX scale, competence system, modern site insight).

4. Competence matrix — map early, support fast

From day one as teamleader I would run a skills survey (self + lead validation). Most people today know only a subset of Lommel systems — there is likely no one with full prior experience on a site this modern in Europe. We must grow specialists internally — external market is scarce.

5. Career paths — specialist pods vs balanced competency

Teamleader choice: not everyone must become identical. Two valid paths:

Path A — Zone / system specialist

  • Own PC-10, PC-20, UPS park, generator chain, etc.
  • Depth over breadth — “go-to” for that installation
  • Best when site enters steady production
  • Risk: silos — countered by rotation + peer review

Path B — Balanced utilities electrician

  • Rotate zones every 6–12 months
  • T-shaped: broad LV + one deepening specialty
  • Best during commissioning / high change
  • Ideal for graduates and international hires building NL+skills

6. Market reality — grow internally

External market

  • Few people with identical modern glass utilities experience
  • Hiring only seniors does not scale
  • Cost and onboarding time high

Internal development (my focus)

  • Competence map from month 1
  • Mentoring + KPI proof of progress
  • Mixed teams break cultural/language silos
  • Utilities becomes learning organisation
Summary for interview: COMEX proved I can supervise through cooperation. At Ciner Glass I add competence engineering for a multicultural, ultra-modern site where specialists must be grown, not only hired.
